BUSINESS MODEL
Residential Intensive Historic Areas Activation Service System - Dayoufang Alley, Nanjing, China

This project was initiated by the Nanjing government. They want to explore a unified mode of operation for this kind of residential intensive historic area. I led the team to conduct part of preliminary field research and user research to improve the basic information of the area for the government. Later I put forward the renovation strategy and the conceptual design of the unified operation platform due to time limitation (a small program run on Wechat).

DATE
April 2020 - June 2020
ROLE
Team Leader
{ CONTEXT }
Nanjing has...
11 Historic blocks
22   Historic feature areas
10  General historic areas
◼ The rapid construction of urbanization has been constantly squeezing historic areas.
◼ Few successful renewal cases of residential intensive historic areas because of their single function and weak economic vitality.
◼ Existing renewal strategies of large-scale, one-time demolition and reconstruction has led to the migration of original residents and the destruction of historical pattern. .

{ PROBLEM STATEMENT }
1| Residents
◼ Wanting to improve living conditions with the help of the government but worrying about the loss of interest due to not being clear about policies (demolition, resettlement, subsidy...)

◼ Unable to identify different departments responsible for different issues.
2| Government
◼ Residents who do not understand the policy do not cooperate with the renovation, which makes it difficult to promote the project.
‍
◼ Many stakeholders are involved, (from industry and universities to research institutions), and the huge information gap results in low work efficiency.
3| Stakeholders
◼ Not clear about the project process and opportunities, so they have a negative attitude and dare not participate rashly.
{ DESIGN OPPORTUNITY }
HMW
take the government's overall planning and residents' self-renovation as the goal, make all social parties actively participate in the renovation, and eliminate information gaps and artificial barriers.

{ DESIGN DETAILS }
Glance, implies that long history gives a glimpse of the historical areas, but it has left a shining mark. A joint platform based on Wechat that helps all social entities to carry out the renovation work of the historical areas at all stages.
Key feature 1 -  Project Process Publicity
◼ All the historical projects in Nanjing are displayed on Wechat.
 
◼ Announce every single project's progress, policies, news, and pilot buildings.
Key feature 2 -  Online Application for Residents' Self-renovation
◼ Encourage all the residents living in a courtyard to renovate together to ensure unity.
‍
◼ Dwelling renovations are verified by the community. Residents can contact the designer and community staff through [consultation].
‍
◼ Historic building renovations are verified by the government.
Key feature 3 -  Business Enter
◼ Merchants can apply to use commercial space or cultural space.
◼ The map will show all spaces. Interested merchants can choose according to their requirements.
◼ Merchants can submit their business plans, and the community and some departments of the government will have further connections with them.

Discussion & Reflection

Limitations

◼ There are many stakeholders involved in the project, and I only participated in the strategy formulation of the initial system map and the conceptual design of the small program of Wechat. So we only tested the concept, which confirmed the necessity of the platform and the government's willingness to adopt and continue to promote it, and did not involve the testing of the specific interaction level.
◼ Most of the residents are vulnerable groups, they have misunderstandings about the policy and because the project involves their own interests, they do not easily trust others, so it is very difficult to carry out the research phase. Almost all residents were evasive at first. During the long chat, the residents gradually let off their guard.
