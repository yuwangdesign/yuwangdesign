RAILTALE

USER RESEARCH | DESIGN THINKING PROCESS
Helping parents experience stress-free and sustainable family journeys with young children.

The purpose of this study is to explore a creative and evidence-based human-centered product concept that is meaningful and potentially transformative for the target user. A critical approach to the selection and application of appropriate data collection and analysis methods and an iterative approach to the design and evaluation of the concept using appropriate tools are the core exercise.

{ INSIGHTS }
◼ Full-time working parents value high-quality parent-child accompany hours during family journeys.
◼ They tend to sacrifice their own needs to keep children entertained and educated during journeys.
◼ Parents feel stressed when keeping attention on the child at all times for safety, health, and playing issues due to children's complex needs, short attention span, and their own unnecessary worries.
{ DESIGN OPPORTUNITY }
There is an opportunity to design a service for full-time working parents who want to keep their child entertained and educated when traveling together but feel tired and stressed when having to keep attention on the child over a long period of time.

Discussion & Reflection

On Methods

◼ Negative experiences are easily overshadowed by parental love.
Memory-related methods will miss a lot of emotional information. So choose objective factors to measure emotions, like division of family and travel responsibilities, and choose an instant information collection method - travel vlogs are very effective iterations of the research methods.

On Mindset

◼ The most important thing I've learned during this project is 'asking your users, asking your stakeholders. if they don’t know what they want, help them find it.' It is different from my past working experience as an architect.

On Ethics

◼ Limitations on children.
Research on children relies mainly on parental descriptions and some theories about cognitive development. Travel vlogs also cannot involve children, but to a certain extent, they restore the environment at that time, which can be regarded as a supplement to the research on children.
